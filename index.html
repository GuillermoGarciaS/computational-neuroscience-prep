The Problem

Raw information is not automatically representation. This site asks what a system must detect, preserve, or use before a signal becomes meaningful for a task.

The Method

Each page turns an abstract idea into a small interactive model: distributions, time windows, detectors, thresholds, channels, and uncertainty.

The Boundary

These models do not explain consciousness. They clarify what computation can explain before the question of subjective experience even begins.

Working thesis

A signal becomes representational only relative to a system that can extract stable structure from it and use that structure for classification, prediction, or transformation. Entropy helps describe uncertainty, but it does not by itself explain meaning or experience.

Boundary condition: detection can produce usable structure without proving that the system understands or experiences anything.
