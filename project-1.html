Project 1 — From Signals to Representation

This synthesis combines the first four experiments into one question: when can a signal begin to function as a representation for a system?

Introduction

Central question

When does raw information become usable structure for classification, prediction, or transformation?

Boundary

Representation here is operational, not mental

This page does not claim that a signal has meaning by itself. It uses a narrower definition: a signal is representational when a system can extract stable structure from it and use that structure to do work.

Model

The model links four ideas from the experiments: entropy, predictability, temporal stability, and detectability. None of them alone is enough. Together, they form a minimal computational test for usable structure.

Representation score = 0.35D + 0.25P + 0.25S + 0.15T

D is detectability, P is predictability, S is stability across observation windows, and T is task usefulness. This is not a universal formula. It is a deliberately simple scoring rule for the simulation.

Signal → measured features → rule → task output

The important movement is not from signal to consciousness. It is from signal to features, and from features to rule-governed use.

Interpretation

A signal becomes representational in this limited model when the system can extract structure that remains stable enough to support an operation. The representation is not the raw signal; it is the compressed set of useful features taken from the signal.

This is why entropy matters but cannot do the whole job. High entropy may hide structure, and low entropy may be trivial. Representation requires a relation between the signal, the detector, and the task.

Limits

Main limitation

This is a computational model of usable structure, not a theory of meaning or consciousness. It shows when a signal can support a task, not why anything would be experienced from the inside.

Core idea

A signal becomes representational only relative to a system that can use its structure.

Warning

Do not confuse detectable structure with meaning or subjective experience.
