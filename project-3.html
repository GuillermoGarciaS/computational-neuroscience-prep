Project #3 — The Boundary of Computational Explanation

After modeling signal, entropy, detection, representation, and integration, this capstone asks what still remains unexplained.

Introduction

Core question

After the computational layers are built, what still separates usable structure from subjective experience?

Page thesis

Computational explanation has a boundary

Computational models can explain how structure becomes detectable, usable, and integrated. They do not automatically explain why or whether anything is experienced from the inside.

Working position

The stronger position is not crude reductionism and not mysticism. Computation explains many functional layers, but subjective experience requires an additional argument.

lim(functional explanation) ≠ proof of lived experience

Core idea

The project should show where computation is powerful and where it stops.

Warning

Do not claim that entropy solves consciousness. That would weaken the whole project.
